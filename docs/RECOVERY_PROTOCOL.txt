SCENARIO 1: THE USB CABLE IS YANKED MID-FLASH
Symptom: Device is hard-bricked. Black screen, no vibration, won't turn on.
Fix: 
1. The device is stuck in a dead boot loop. You must force it back into EDL (Qualcomm) or BROM (MediaTek).
2. Open the back cover and short the hardware Test Points on the motherboard, then plug in the USB.
3. Once Phoenix Forge detects the raw port, go to the "Maintenance" tab and click "Restore from Shadow Rollback." 
4. Select the exact timestamp of the backup you made 5 minutes ago. Let the engine rewrite the NVRAM.

SCENARIO 2: QUALCOMM AUTHENTICATION REJECTED
Symptom: Dashboard says "Cloud Vault Rejected Handshake."
Fix: 
1. Check your internet connection. The token spooler requires an active connection to your RSA server.
2. Verify your Technician ID hasn't been revoked in the master cloud dashboard.

SCENARIO 3: MEDIATEK BOOTROM GLITCH FAILS
Symptom: Terminal says "USB Pipe Error" but the Download Agent refuses to inject.
Fix: Timing is everything on the TOCTOU glitch. 
1. Unplug the phone. 
2. Disconnect the battery physically for 5 seconds. Reconnect it.
3. Hold Volume UP + Volume DOWN simultaneously.
4. Plug in the USB and immediately click "Execute Glitch" in the UI.
